Overview

Lately it feels like there is a new AI startup getting funded every day. The industry is evolving so quickly that I felt like I needed to find a way to make sense of what's working and what isn't.

This started off as a tool to figure out where the startups that received funding from 2023 to 2025 are right now, and to use that to understand if there are larger shifts taking place.

The companies are mostly YC incubees. Each company is scored on seven public signals (website uptime, Wayback freshness, blog cadence, GitHub activity, hiring pulse, trusted news coverage, and curated notes), and the score maps to one of six status buckets from Thriving at the top to Likely Dead at the bottom.

Every company also carries two classification tags:

  • Sector names the vertical the company serves, such as Healthcare, Finance, Legal, Education, or Real Estate.
  • When a company's product is sold across verticals, for example developer tooling, agent infrastructure, or general LLM platforms, the sector is Cross-industry.
  • Function names where the company sits in the AI stack: Foundation Models, ML Infrastructure, Data Infrastructure, AI Agent, Copilot or Assistant, Generative Product, Analytics and Decisioning, or Research Lab.

The shape of the cohort today:

  • 26 Thriving
  • 82 Healthy
  • 384 on the Watchlist
  • 11 Dormant
  • 49 Likely Dead
  • 25 Pivoted or Absorbed
  • Most of the change between weekly runs happens inside the Watchlist, which is why the month-over-month delta is the real story.

Headline

Most AI startups from the 2023 to 2025 wave are neither thriving nor dead. 384 of the 577 tracked companies (67%) sit in the Watchlist middle, where the monthly delta is the real signal rather than today's score.

How to read this page

  • Today's snapshot: a ring chart of all 577 companies broken down by status bucket, with a matching legend.
  • Sector × Function: a grid showing which sectors pair with which functions across the cohort. Darker cells are more companies.
  • By status: six sections, one per bucket. Each section contains two bar charts (sector and function mix inside the bucket), a top 5 scorecard of the strongest-scoring companies, a short auto-generated insight paragraph, and a filterable company list with dropdowns for sector and function.

The full methodology, code, and raw dataset are in the GitHub repo.
